## SLIDE 5: CYBERSECURITY CHALLENGES IN MOZAMBIQUE

### Title
**The Current Cybersecurity Landscape in Mozambique**

### Key Challenges
- **Critical Infrastructure Vulnerability:** Limited detection and response capabilities for threats to power, water, communications
- **Government & Ministry Systems:** Aging infrastructure, inconsistent security practices, legacy systems difficult to defend
- **Financial Sector Exposure:** Growing cyber fraud targeting banks, mobile money platforms, and payment systems
- **Limited Cyber Intelligence:** No centralized threat monitoring or early-warning capabilities
- **SME & Micro-Business Risk:** Widespread susceptibility to ransomware, fraud, and data theft
- **Cybercriminal Activity:** Active networks exploiting Mozambique's digital vulnerabilities; limited ability to track or prosecute
- **Human Factor:** Low cyber hygiene awareness across government, business, and general population
- **Reputational Impact:** Mozambique perceived globally as a high-risk digital environment

### Consequences
- Loss of investor confidence in digital services
- Increased financial losses to cybercrime
- Compromised government security
- Reduced competitiveness in global digital economy

## SLIDE 6: WHY CYBERSECURITY AWARENESS MATTERS

### Title
**The Awareness Imperative: Building a Culture of Cyber Responsibility**

### The Human Factor
- **85% of breaches** involve human error or social engineering
- **Untrained users** are the weakest link in any security system
- **Government officials** handling sensitive data without cyber hygiene training
- **Business leaders** making decisions without understanding cyber risk
- **General population** vulnerable to fraud, phishing, identity theft

### Benefits of Awareness Programs
- ✓ Reduces vulnerability to social engineering attacks by 60-80%
- ✓ Empowers employees to recognize and report threats
- ✓ Creates a culture where "security is everyone's job"
- ✓ Protects institutional knowledge and sensitive data
- ✓ Builds citizen resilience against cybercrime
- ✓ Transforms cybersecurity from an IT issue to a national priority

### Target Audiences
1. **Government** → Ministry staff, officials, decision-makers
2. **Critical Infrastructure** → Energy, water, communications operators
3. **Financial Sector** → Banks, payment platforms, fintech companies
4. **Private Sector** → Large corporations, supply chain partners
5. **SME Ecosystem** → Small and medium enterprises, micro-businesses
6. **General Public** → Citizens, young people, digital users

                    ↓
